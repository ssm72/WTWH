About Henna

Henna is a commonly used plant product that can be ground into a paste and used as a a temporary tattoo or paint product. Henna is common in many different cultures and makes for an attractive, dark and semi-permanent tattoo. However, there are a number of benefits that henna can provide to you that aren't related to the appearance of the tattoo or the design on your skin. The art of henna (called mehndi in Hindi & Urdu) has been polished for over 5000 years in Pakistan, India, Africa and the Middle East. There is some documentation that it is in excess of 9000 years of age. Because henna has common cooling properties, individuals of the desert, for a long time, have been utilizing henna to chill off their bodies.

Types of Henna

There are different types of dye ranging from black henna to herbal henna. Here are five different types of henna dye.

Black Henna:Henna that manufacturers have added black dye to in order to create darker tattoos or black hair color.
Red Henna:Often to dye hair. It is a great natural alternative to regular hair dye, but it is important to know that not everybody's hair will turn out exactly the same since the dye is a natural product.
Natural Henna:Most commonly used for temporary body art. It is also used for hair conditioning and dying.
Herbal Henna:Used for staining the skin for temporary tattoos as well as a treatment for the hair.

Side-Effects

While using henna one should also be aware of the possible side effects which are more so in the use of black henna which is known to result in rashes, skin allergies and reactions. Henna seems to be safe for most adults when used on the skin or hair. It can cause some side effects such as inflammation of the skin (dermatitis) including redness, itching, burning, swelling, scaling, broken skin, blisters, and scarring of the skin. Rarely, allergic reactions can occur such as hives, runny nose, wheezing, and asthma. Henna is considered to be UNSAFE when taken by mouth. Accidentally swallowing henna requires prompt medical attention. It can cause stomach upset and other side effects.

Benefits

The henna plant is not only used for benefits to the appearance of your skin. It also has a number of nutrients and other benefits in it which can help to alter the qualities of your skin in other ways too. It helps in Improving Defence Against Infections, Cooling Skin, Pain Reliever, Scalp Benefit, and many more. Some other benefits of henna are:
Liver disorder: Henna is considered to be a highly beneficial medicinal plant and hence the bark of the henna plant is known to be effective in the treatment of the enlargement of the liver as well as jaundice.
Henna for Hair: One of the key benefits of henna for hair is that it acts as a natural remedy for hair loss and is also excellent for maintaining overall hair quality.
